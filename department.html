Our Departments

Touching lifes through quality healthcare

1. Geriatrics
(Elderly Care)

The Geriatrics Department is dedicated exclusively to the healthcare needs of older adults aged 60 years and above. We recognize that ageing comes with unique medical, physical, emotional, and social challenges that require specialized attention and compassionate care.
Our services address chronic illnesses, memory changes, mobility problems, chronic pain, nutritional concerns, and age-related decline in physical and cognitive function. We manage multiple conditions concurrently, ensuring treatments are safe, appropriate, and aligned with each patient’s overall wellbeing and life goals.
Care is holistic and patient-centered, emphasizing dignity, comfort, and independence. Families and caregivers are actively involved in care planning to ensure continuity and support beyond the hospital setting. Our geriatric services aim to enhance daily functioning, reduce hospitalizations, and promote healthy ageing in a safe and supportive environment.

2. Doctors Consultation

Our Doctors’ Consultation services provide patients with access to experienced medical professionals across multiple specialties. Consultations focus on accurate diagnosis, clear communication, and individualized treatment planning.
Patients receive thorough medical assessments, review of test results, and guidance on treatment options. Special attention is given to elderly patients who may present with multiple or complex health conditions.
Our goal is to build long-term doctor–patient relationships that promote trust, continuity of care, and improved health outcomes.

3. General Medicine / Internal Medicine

Our General Medicine (Internal Medicine) department forms the foundation of comprehensive adult healthcare within the hospital. We specialize in the diagnosis, treatment, and long-term management of a wide range of acute and chronic medical conditions affecting adults and the elderly. These include diabetes, hypertension, asthma, kidney disease, lung disease, epilepsy, arthritis, heart failure, and stroke follow-up care.
Patients receive thorough medical evaluations that include detailed history-taking, physical examinations, and appropriate laboratory and diagnostic investigations. Treatment plans are individualized to address not only the illness itself but also contributing lifestyle factors, medication adherence, and long-term disease control. Our goal is to stabilize health conditions, prevent complications, and reduce avoidable hospital admissions.
For elderly patients, care is delivered with particular attention to multiple co-existing conditions, medication interactions, mobility limitations, and age-related vulnerability. Continuous follow-up, preventive screening, and patient education are central to maintaining independence and improving quality of life.

4. Nursing Services

Our Nursing Services deliver continuous, compassionate, and professional care across outpatient, inpatient, and home-based settings. Nurses play a critical role in patient monitoring, medication administration, wound care, and daily support.
Elderly patients benefit from attentive nursing care that prioritizes comfort, dignity, safety, and early detection of complications.
The nursing team works closely with doctors and families to ensure coordinated, high-quality care throughout the patient’s healthcare journey.

5. Medication & Prescriptions

This service ensures safe, accurate, and timely prescribing of medications based on each patient’s diagnosis and clinical needs.
Prescriptions are carefully reviewed to minimize drug interactions, particularly for elderly patients managing multiple conditions. Patients receive clear instructions on medication use and follow-up.
Proper medication management supports treatment effectiveness, reduces adverse effects, and improves long-term health outcomes.

6. Preventive Health Check-ups

Preventive Health Check-Ups focus on early detection and prevention of disease through regular screening and medical assessments.
Packages may include physical examinations, laboratory tests, imaging, and lifestyle assessments tailored to age and risk factors.
For elderly patients, preventive care helps identify potential health issues early, supports healthy aging, and reduces avoidable hospital admissions.

7. Maternity care

Our Maternity Care services provide comprehensive support throughout pregnancy, childbirth, and postnatal recovery.
Care includes antenatal visits, labor and delivery support, postnatal monitoring, and education for new mothers. Services are delivered in a safe, supportive environment.
We emphasize maternal safety, healthy newborn outcomes, and emotional support for families during this important life stage.

8. Antenatal care

Our antenatal care services are designed to support mothers throughout pregnancy, ensuring the health and safety of both mother and baby. We provide comprehensive prenatal monitoring that includes routine check-ups, laboratory testing, ultrasound imaging, blood pressure monitoring, and screening for pregnancy-related complications.
Expectant mothers receive education on nutrition, physical activity, danger signs during pregnancy, birth preparedness, and newborn care. Early identification of risks allows for timely intervention and referral when necessary.
Our team creates a supportive and reassuring environment, guiding mothers through each stage of pregnancy while promoting positive outcomes and confidence in maternal health.

9. Family planning

Our family planning services offer confidential, respectful, and comprehensive reproductive healthcare to individuals and couples. We provide counseling on a wide range of modern contraceptive options, helping clients make informed decisions based on health needs, family goals, and personal preferences.
Services include method selection, education on correct use, follow-up visits, and management of side effects. We emphasize safety, effectiveness, and long-term satisfaction with chosen family planning methods.
By promoting responsible family planning, we support maternal health, child wellbeing, and overall family stability across all stages of life.

10. Minor Medical Procedures

We offer a range of Minor Medical Procedures performed safely by qualified healthcare professionals.
These include wound suturing, abscess drainage, dressing changes, catheter insertion, and other low-risk interventions.
Minor procedures are carried out efficiently to reduce discomfort, prevent complications, and allow patients to recover quickly.

11. Health Education & Counseling

Health Education and Counseling empower patients and families with knowledge to manage their health effectively.
Topics include disease management, medication adherence, nutrition, physical activity, mental wellbeing, and lifestyle modification.
Education is especially important for elderly patients and caregivers, helping them make informed decisions and maintain independence.

1. 24-hour emergency response
and patient stabilization

Our 24-hour Emergency Response service provides immediate medical attention for acute illnesses, injuries, and life-threatening conditions.
Patients receive rapid assessment, stabilization, and initiation of treatment by trained emergency staff.
Prompt emergency care is critical for elderly patients, where early intervention can significantly improve survival and recovery outcomes.

2. Intensive Care Unit (ICU)
and High Dependency Unit (HDU)

The ICU and HDU provide advanced care for critically ill patients requiring close monitoring and specialized medical support.
Services include continuous vital sign monitoring, ventilatory support, advanced medication therapy, and multidisciplinary care.
Special consideration is given to elderly patients to balance intensive treatment with comfort, dignity, and overall wellbeing.

3. Cardiac and respiratory
emergency management

This service focuses on the rapid diagnosis and treatment of heart and lung emergencies such as heart attacks, heart failure exacerbations, asthma attacks, and respiratory distress.
Timely intervention, continuous monitoring, and coordinated care improve patient outcomes and reduce complications.
Elderly patients benefit from early recognition and specialized management of cardiac and respiratory emergencies.

4. Ambulance and patient transfer
services

Our Ambulance and Patient Transfer Services ensure safe and timely transportation of patients during emergencies or between healthcare facilities.
Ambulances are equipped with essential medical equipment and staffed by trained personnel to provide care en route.
This service supports continuity of care and rapid access to life-saving interventions when needed.

5. Post-emergency rehabilitation
and follow-up care

Post-Emergency Rehabilitation and Follow-Up Care support patients as they recover from serious illness, surgery, or emergency treatment.
Services include medical reviews, physiotherapy, medication adjustments, and long-term care planning.
For elderly patients, structured follow-up reduces readmissions, restores function, and improves quality of life after hospitalization.

6. Close monitoring of critically Ill patients

We provide continuous and intensive monitoring for patients whose conditions require close medical observation. This service is essential for individuals with unstable vital signs, severe infections, respiratory distress, or complications from chronic illnesses.
Patients are monitored using advanced equipment that tracks heart rate, blood pressure, oxygen levels, and neurological status. Medical and nursing teams remain on standby to respond immediately to any deterioration.
This level of monitoring ensures early detection of complications, timely interventions, and improved survival outcomes, especially for elderly and high-risk patients.

8. Inpatient critical care for elderly
and general patients

Our inpatient critical care services offer advanced medical support for patients with life-threatening or complex medical conditions. We cater to both elderly and general patients who require constant supervision, specialized treatment, and intensive nursing care.
Treatment includes respiratory support, cardiac monitoring, medication management, nutritional support, and infection control. Care plans are individualized, taking into account age-related vulnerabilities, existing chronic conditions, and overall prognosis.
The goal is stabilization, recovery, and safe transition to lower levels of care or rehabilitation, while preserving dignity and comfort at all stages of treatment.

9. Acute stroke management

Our Acute Stroke Management service provides rapid, coordinated, and life-saving care for patients presenting with signs of stroke. Time is critical in stroke treatment, and our team is trained to act swiftly to reduce brain damage and improve recovery outcomes.
Patients undergo immediate clinical assessment, neurological evaluation, and diagnostic imaging to determine the type and severity of stroke. Treatment focuses on stabilizing vital functions, managing blood pressure, preventing complications, and initiating appropriate medical interventions as early as possible.
Following the acute phase, patients receive structured follow-up care, rehabilitation planning, and long-term management to reduce the risk of recurrence and support functional recovery, particularly for elderly patients who are at higher risk.

1. General Surgery

The General Surgery department provides safe, effective, and patient-centered surgical care for a wide range of conditions. Our surgical team performs both elective and emergency procedures using evidence-based techniques and strict safety protocols.
Services include abdominal surgeries, soft tissue procedures, minor surgical interventions, and post-operative wound management. Patients receive comprehensive pre-operative assessments and structured post-operative care to ensure optimal recovery.
Special consideration is given to elderly patients through careful risk assessment, anesthesia planning, pain management, and post-surgical monitoring to minimize complications.

2. Orthopedics

Our Orthopedics service focuses on the diagnosis, treatment, and rehabilitation of conditions affecting bones, joints, muscles, and the musculoskeletal system. We manage fractures, arthritis, joint pain, mobility disorders, and age-related bone conditions.
Treatment options range from conservative management such as medication and physiotherapy to surgical intervention when necessary. Rehabilitation and mobility restoration are central components of care.
For elderly patients, emphasis is placed on fall prevention, pain control, functional independence, and improving overall quality of life.

3. ENT Surgery

Our ENT (Ear, Nose, and Throat) Surgery services address conditions affecting hearing, breathing, speech, and balance. We manage infections, growths, obstructions, and chronic ENT disorders through both medical and surgical approaches.
Procedures are performed with precision and care, supported by thorough pre-operative evaluation and post-operative follow-up. Patients receive education on recovery, medication use, and preventive care.
ENT services are particularly beneficial for elderly patients who may experience hearing loss, chronic sinus issues, or swallowing difficulties that impact daily living.

4. Reconstructive Surgery

Reconstructive Surgery focuses on restoring function, appearance, and quality of life following injury, disease, or surgical procedures. Our services address trauma-related defects, post-surgical reconstruction, and correction of functional impairments.
Each case is approached with detailed planning, patient counseling, and individualized surgical strategies. Recovery includes close monitoring and rehabilitation support.
This service plays a vital role in improving physical function, self-esteem, and long-term wellbeing for patients of all ages.

1. Obstetrics and Gynecology management

Our Obstetrics and Gynecology services provide comprehensive care for women across all stages of life. We manage pregnancy, childbirth, gynecological conditions, hormonal disorders, and reproductive health concerns.
Services include antenatal care, delivery support, postnatal follow-up, menstrual disorder management, and treatment of gynecological conditions. Patient safety, comfort, and confidentiality are prioritized at all times.
Women receive personalized care supported by medical expertise, modern diagnostics, and continuous monitoring.

2. Physiotherapy and rehabilitation care

Physiotherapy and rehabilitation services are designed to restore movement, strength, and functional independence after illness, injury, surgery, or prolonged hospitalization.
Our therapists develop individualized rehabilitation plans that include exercise therapy, mobility training, pain management, and functional re-education. Progress is closely monitored to ensure safe and effective recovery.
Elderly patients benefit greatly from rehabilitation services that help prevent falls, maintain independence, and improve overall quality of life.

3. Palliative care

Our Palliative Care services focus on improving comfort, dignity, and quality of life for patients with serious or life-limiting illnesses. Care is centered on pain relief, symptom control, emotional support, and compassionate communication.
We support both patients and families through medical, psychological, and spiritual care, ensuring that treatment aligns with patient values and preferences.
Palliative care can be provided alongside curative treatment or as primary care when cure is no longer possible, with special attention to elderly patients and those with chronic conditions.

4. Home-based care

Our Home-Based Care service brings professional medical and nursing care directly to patients in the comfort of their homes. This service is ideal for elderly patients, individuals with limited mobility, and those recovering from illness or surgery.
Care includes medical reviews, nursing procedures, medication administration, wound care, physiotherapy support, and monitoring of chronic conditions. Each care plan is tailored to the patient’s medical needs, living environment, and family support system.
Home-based care promotes comfort, dignity, independence, and continuity of care while reducing unnecessary hospital visits and admissions.

1. General laboratory tests

We offer a wide range of general laboratory tests that support accurate diagnosis, treatment planning, and ongoing patient care. These tests include routine blood tests, urine analysis, stool examinations, and basic biochemical investigations.
Our laboratory operates under strict quality and safety standards to ensure reliable and timely results. Tests are conducted using modern equipment and validated procedures.
General laboratory testing is essential for preventive care, disease detection, and monitoring of both acute and chronic conditions, particularly in elderly patients.

2. Specialized laboratory tests

Specialized laboratory testing provides advanced diagnostic insight for complex and chronic medical conditions. These tests support early detection, disease staging, and treatment evaluation.
Services include hormonal assays, cardiac markers, infection screening, renal and liver function tests, and other specialized investigations as clinically indicated.
Specialized tests are interpreted in close collaboration with clinicians to ensure accurate diagnosis and effective patient management.

3. Routine and follow-up tests for
chronic disease management

This service focuses on continuous monitoring of patients living with long-term medical conditions such as diabetes, hypertension, kidney disease, heart failure, and respiratory illnesses.
Regular testing helps track disease progression, assess treatment effectiveness, and identify complications early. Results guide timely adjustments to medication and care plans.
Routine monitoring is especially important for elderly patients, enabling safer long-term care and improved quality of life.

1. ECG (Electrocardiography)

Electrocardiography (ECG) is used to assess the electrical activity of the heart and detect abnormalities such as arrhythmias, heart attacks, and structural heart conditions.
The test is quick, non-invasive, and performed by trained professionals using reliable equipment. Results are reviewed by clinicians for prompt clinical decision-making.
ECG testing is essential for cardiac assessment, pre-operative evaluation, and monitoring of elderly patients with heart disease.

2. EEG (Electroence
phalography)

EEG testing records electrical activity in the brain and is used to diagnose and monitor neurological conditions such as epilepsy, seizures, and altered consciousness.
The procedure is painless and conducted in a controlled environment to ensure accurate readings. Results assist clinicians in developing appropriate treatment plans.
EEG services support comprehensive neurological care for both elderly and general patients.

3. Echocardiography

Echocardiography uses ultrasound technology to create detailed images of the heart’s structure and function. It helps assess heart valves, chambers, blood flow, and pumping efficiency.
This test is critical in diagnosing heart failure, valve disorders, and other cardiovascular conditions. It is non-invasive and safe for all age groups.
Elderly patients benefit greatly from echocardiography as part of routine cardiac evaluation and chronic disease management.

4. Lung Function Tests

Lung Function Tests assess how well the lungs work by measuring airflow, lung capacity, and oxygen exchange. These tests help diagnose and manage asthma, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), and other respiratory conditions.
Testing is performed in a controlled setting with clear guidance to ensure accurate results. Findings guide treatment plans and monitor disease progression.
This service is particularly valuable for elderly patients and individuals with chronic respiratory illnesses.

1. Inpatient care

Our inpatient care services provide structured, round-the-clock medical and nursing support for patients who require hospital admission. Whether admitted for medical management, post-surgical recovery, or close observation, patients receive coordinated care from a multidisciplinary healthcare team.
Each inpatient receives daily medical reviews, continuous nursing monitoring, medication administration, nutritional support, and rehabilitation services when needed. Care plans are regularly updated based on patient progress and response to treatment.
Elderly patients benefit from enhanced safety measures, including fall prevention strategies, pressure sore prevention, infection control, and cognitive monitoring. Our inpatient services focus on recovery, comfort, and safe discharge planning to ensure patients transition smoothly back to home or continued care.

1. 24-hour pharmacy

Our 24-hour pharmacy ensures continuous access to essential medications at all times, supporting both inpatients and outpatients.
The pharmacy is stocked with a wide range of prescription medicines, emergency drugs, and chronic disease medications. Pharmacists work closely with clinicians to ensure accurate dispensing and medication safety.
Round-the-clock access is especially critical for elderly patients and emergency cases where timely medication can be life-saving.

2. Medication counseling
(critical for elderly patients on multiple drugs)

Medication counseling services focus on safe and effective use of medicines, particularly for elderly patients who may be on multiple medications.
Pharmacists provide clear guidance on dosage, timing, possible side effects, drug interactions, and adherence strategies. Patients and caregivers are encouraged to ask questions and participate actively in care decisions.
This service reduces medication errors, improves treatment outcomes, and enhances patient confidence and safety.
